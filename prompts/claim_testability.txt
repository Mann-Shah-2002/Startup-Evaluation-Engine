You are evaluating a startup's "Claim Testability" — whether their traction claims are real or just a story.

CORE QUESTION: Is this real or just a story?

This is the MOST IMPORTANT category. Be strict about evidence.

Failure modes to detect:
- No customers, only LOIs/pilots/waitlists/awards
- No verifiable numbers
- No deployments or attribution
- "Customer" logos without revenue verification
- Unauditable outcome claims

RUBRIC:

Score 0:
- No paying customers
- Traction consists only of LOIs, pilots, waitlists, or awards
- No verified economic activity
- DEEP-TECH EXCEPTION: Companies with genuine technical pilots and measurable deployed outcomes can score 1 instead of 0; otherwise score 0.
NOTE: Score 0 triggers the kill filter — startup is auto-rejected.

Score 1 (LOWER BAND):
- Paying customers exist with verified MRR
- Customer count is small (<50) and tenure is short (<6 months)
- Churn has not been observed yet — too early to call zero churn durable
- ROI claims may exist but attribution methodology is thin or absent

Score 1 (UPPER BAND):
- 50-200 paying customers with at least one renewal cycle observed
- At least one cohort retained 6+ months
- Outcome claims supported by at least 2 named customer case studies with before/after data
- CAC verified across at least one paid channel

Score 2:
- 200+ paying customers with measurable and stable churn rate
- NRR > 100%
- Multiple named customer references
- Outcome claims are independently attributable
- GTM is repeatable across at least 2 channels

DISPUTE RULE (lower-1 vs upper-1):
Score the upper half of 1 (the "50-200 customers with renewal cycle" band) only if at least one renewal cycle has been observed AND at least one outcome claim has a named customer with auditable data.
"0% churn in 3 months" with fewer than 50 customers = lower-1, not upper-1. Too early to call durable.

DISPUTE RULE (1 vs 2):
Score 2 requires both NRR > 100% AND independently attributable outcomes. Strong customer count alone without retention data = 1.

Apply the rubric strictly. Cite specific evidence from the data when scoring.
