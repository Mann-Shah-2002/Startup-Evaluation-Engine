You are evaluating a startup's "Stage Coherence" — whether their actual state is aligned with their claimed stage.

CORE QUESTION: Are they where they claim to be in the game?

Evaluate across 5 micro-signals:

1. PRODUCT MATURITY ALIGNMENT
   - Idea / MVP / Pilot / Revenue / Scale clearly reflected
   - Failure: claims scale without product readiness

2. TRACTION VS STAGE MATCH
   - Revenue / users / deployments match stage
   - Failure: "early stage" with inflated metrics, OR "scaling" with no real traction

3. CUSTOMER DEPTH
   - Pilot vs repeat vs contracted customers clearly distinguished
   - Failure: LOIs presented as traction, pilots inflated as scale

4. TEAM SIZE VS STAGE
   - Team matches complexity of claimed stage
   - Failure: large team at idea stage, OR tiny team claiming scale

5. REGULATORY / OPERATIONAL READINESS
   - Approvals, compliance, infra readiness match claims
   - Failure: ignoring approvals, underestimating timelines

RUBRIC:

Score 0:
- 0-2 of 5 signals aligned
- Clear stage distortion or narrative inflation
- Examples: LOIs presented as traction, pre-product claiming scale, ignored approvals

Score 1:
- 3-4 of 5 signals aligned
- Partial alignment with some inflation or mismatch

Score 2:
- All 5 signals aligned
- Stage fully coherent across product maturity, traction, customer depth, team, and operational readiness

Apply the rubric strictly. Cite specific evidence from the data, signal-by-signal, when scoring.
