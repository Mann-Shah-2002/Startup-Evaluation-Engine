You are evaluating a startup's "Sector Coherence" — whether they understand how their market actually works.

CORE QUESTION: Do they understand how the game is played in their market?

Evaluate across 5 micro-signals:

1. BUYER CLARITY
   - Clear ICP (who pays, who uses)
   - Decision-maker understood
   - Failure: "everyone is customer", confusion between user vs payer

2. REVENUE MODEL FIT
   - Matches sector norms (SaaS, FMCG, Gov capex, etc.)
   - Monetization path is realistic
   - Failure: monetization disconnected from behavior, delayed or unclear revenue logic

3. DISTRIBUTION LOGIC
   - How product reaches customer is defined
   - Channel strategy matches sector reality
   - Failure: "we will go viral", no acquisition path

4. COMPETITIVE AWARENESS
   - Real competitors named
   - Differentiation is structural, not cosmetic
   - Failure: "no competition", surface-level comparison

5. MARKET STRUCTURE UNDERSTANDING
   - Procurement cycles, pricing power, switching cost understood
   - Failure: ignores friction (govt cycles, CAC, shelf space, etc.)

RUBRIC:

Score 0:
- 0-2 of 5 signals clear
- Misunderstood market or broken revenue logic

Score 1:
- 3-4 of 5 signals clear
- Partial understanding with identifiable gaps

Score 2:
- All 5 signals clear
- Structural understanding demonstrated across buyer, monetization, distribution, competition, and market mechanics

SECTOR CYCLE MODIFIER:
Does not change the base score. However:
- Trending sectors do not earn score inflation for weak fundamentals
- Saturated sectors require differentiation to be structural (not cosmetic) to score 2

Apply the rubric strictly. Cite specific evidence from the data, signal-by-signal, when scoring.
