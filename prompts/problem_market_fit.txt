You are evaluating a startup's "Problem × Market Fit" — whether the problem is real and there is demonstrated market pull.

CORE QUESTION: Is the problem real and is there demonstrated pull?

This is a COMBINED judgment across two dimensions:
- Problem framing quality (specificity, evidence, buyer authority)
- Market pull evidence (inbound, retention, pricing power)

RUBRIC:

Score 0:
- Problem is vague, generic, or fabricated
- Examples: "Everyone needs X" / "inefficiency in Y industry" without specifics on who hurts, how much, or how often
- Solution-looking-for-a-problem framing
- No market pull signals: no inbound demand, no repeat purchase, no referrals, no retention evidence
- Buyer willingness to pay is assumed, not demonstrated

Score 1:
- Problem is real and identifiable but framing is weak
- Stated generically, not quantified in time/money/workflow terms, OR not tied to a specific buyer persona with budget authority
- Solution addresses the problem but market pull evidence is thin
- Some paid users exist but without strong signals (low referral rate, unclear retention, no inbound)
OR
- Strong pull exists but the team cannot articulate WHY it's working — risk of unsystematic growth

Score 2:
- Problem is specific, acute, and quantified with evidence (stated cost, time loss, workflow break, compliance risk)
- Buyer persona is explicit and has budget authority
- Solution shape directly maps to the stated pain
- Clear market pull signals: inbound demand, measurable referral behavior, strong retention/NRR, OR willingness-to-pay demonstrated through pricing power (not discounting to close)

DISPUTE RULE (1 vs 2):
Score 2 requires both sides:
- A crisp, quantified problem AND demonstrated market pull
- Great problem framing with weak pull = 1 (hypothesis, not validation)
- Strong pull with vague problem framing = 1 (team can't systematize what's working, fragile to scale)

Apply the rubric strictly. Cite specific evidence from the data when scoring.
