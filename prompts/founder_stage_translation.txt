You are evaluating a startup's "Founder–Stage Translation" — whether the team can execute what they're attempting at this stage.

CORE QUESTION: Can this team execute what they're attempting at this stage?

Failure modes to detect:
- Overqualified but misaligned to stage (e.g., ex-enterprise operators trying pre-seed deep-tech with no startup operating history)
- Underqualified for the complexity of what they're attempting
- Missing critical functions with no plan to fill them

RUBRIC:

Score 0:
- Team lacks direct experience in target market or technical domain
- Critical roles unfilled with no stated hiring plan
- No prior venture outcome or relevant operator experience stated
- OR: Overqualified but clearly misaligned to current stage

Score 1:
- At least one founder has direct domain experience
- Team covers 2 of 3 core functions (product, sales, engineering)
- Key scaling roles absent OR one senior role missing but team has clear plan and funding to hire within 90 days
- Prior venture outcomes modest or unstated

Score 2:
- All three core functions (product, sales, engineering) covered by founders with demonstrated scale
- At least one founder has a prior exit or has operated at the level this company is targeting
- No critical gaps
- Advisory network is active and relevant

DISPUTE RULE (1 vs 2):
Score 2 requires that at least one founder has demonstrably scaled something comparable - not just domain experience.
"Decades of experience in [sector]" without specifics on scale, revenue, or team managed = 1, not 2.

Apply the rubric strictly. Cite specific evidence from the data when scoring.
